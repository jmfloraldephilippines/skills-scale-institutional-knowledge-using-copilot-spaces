# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead / Test Manager

### Role Summary
QA Leads define testing strategy, ensure quality standards are met, and validate that features meet acceptance criteria before release. They bridge product requirements and technical quality, ensuring customer satisfaction and compliance.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain comprehensive test plans aligned with feature scope
- Define and refine acceptance criteria with Product and Development teams
- Coordinate manual and automated testing efforts across the team
- Identify, triage, and track defects; collaborate with developers on fixes
- Validate compliance with quality, security, and regulatory standards
- Maintain test automation infrastructure and track coverage metrics
- Plan and execute smoke tests for critical flows before release

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal defects reaching production
- Reduce regression risks through comprehensive and maintainable test coverage
- Identify quality gaps early in the development cycle to reduce rework
- Balance speed with thoroughness in release readiness

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement (defining test scope)
- Defect triage meetings and prioritization
- Test status reports and quality dashboards
- Release readiness reviews and go/no-go decisions

---

## Technical Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Architects design system and solution architectures, ensure scalability and maintainability, and guide technical trade-off decisions. They provide strategic technical guidance across projects and teams.

### Responsibilities
- Define high-level system design and technology stack recommendations
- Review feature designs for architectural alignment and technical feasibility
- Identify technical risks, dependencies, and integration points
- Collaborate on API contracts, interface design, and integration strategies
- Mentor developers on architectural patterns, best practices, and standards
- Guide refactoring priorities and technical debt management
- Support capacity planning and performance optimization initiatives

### Goals
- Build scalable, maintainable systems aligned with business goals
- Minimize technical debt and reduce rework from architectural missteps
- Ensure consistency, reusability, and interoperability across components and services
- Reduce long-term maintenance costs through sound design decisions

### Typical Communication
- Architecture design reviews and technical design documents
- Sprint planning (technical feasibility assessment and dependency mapping)
- Cross-team integration planning and requirements
- Code reviews and technical mentoring sessions
- Technology selection and vendor evaluation discussions

---

## Design / UX Lead

### Role Summary
Design/UX Leads ensure features are usable, accessible, and aligned with product vision through user-centered design practices. They advocate for the user experience throughout the product development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research, usability testing, and accessibility audits
- Create wireframes, prototypes, design specifications, and user flows
- Ensure accessibility standards (WCAG) and usability best practices are met
- Collaborate with Product and Development on design feasibility and implementation
- Iterate designs based on user feedback, testing results, and business requirements
- Maintain design systems, component libraries, and design documentation
- Review implementation against design specifications and provide feedback

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences that delight customers
- Reduce user friction, support costs, and feature abandonment
- Maintain visual and interaction consistency across the product
- Build usability into every feature from conception through release

### Typical Communication
- User research and usability test reviews with stakeholders
- Design critique sessions and feedback cycles
- Sprint planning (design scope and requirements definition)
- Release and demo reviews to ensure design fidelity in implementation
- Design system updates and component documentation

---

## DevOps / Infrastructure Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers design, deploy, and maintain infrastructure and CI/CD pipelines to enable reliable, scalable, and secure releases. They optimize deployment processes and system reliability.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain production, staging, and development infrastructure
- Build, optimize, and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage secrets, security credentials, and access controls
- Monitor system performance, uptime, error rates, and key metrics
- Support incident response, troubleshooting, and rollback procedures
- Optimize infrastructure costs and resource utilization
- Document runbooks and disaster recovery procedures

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and safe deployments with minimal manual effort
- Maximize system availability, performance, and customer experience
- Reduce deployment overhead, human error, and time-to-recovery
- Build infrastructure that scales and adapts to business needs

### Typical Communication
- Release planning and deployment coordination
- Incident response and post-mortem analysis
- Infrastructure reviews and optimization discussions
- Performance monitoring, alerts, and capacity planning
- Security and compliance reviews related to infrastructure

---

## Security / Compliance Officer

### Role Summary
Security and Compliance Officers ensure projects meet security standards, regulatory requirements, and organizational policies. They are guardians of data protection and risk management.

### Responsibilities
- Review projects for security risks, threats, and compliance requirements
- Define security acceptance criteria and testing standards
- Conduct security reviews, threat assessments, and penetration testing
- Advise on data privacy, encryption, access controls, and secure development practices
- Manage security scanning, vulnerability remediation, and patch management
- Support incident response, investigations, and audit requirements
- Maintain security documentation and compliance evidence

### Goals
- Reduce security and compliance risks to acceptable levels
- Ensure data protection, regulatory alignment, and organizational policy adherence
- Build customer trust through transparent, secure practices
- Embed security into the development process from the start

### Typical Communication
- Project initiation and risk reviews
- Security design reviews and threat modeling sessions
- Incident response and investigations
- Audit and compliance reporting
- Security training and awareness initiatives

---

## Agile Coach / Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Agile Coaches facilitate team practices, remove impediments, and drive continuous improvement in delivery processes. They enable high-performing teams and optimize workflow efficiency.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and help resolve team blockers, process impediments, and dependencies
- Track and optimize team velocity, cycle time, and delivery predictability
- Coach teams on Agile practices, principles, and ceremonies
- Support process improvements based on retrospective feedback and metrics
- Coordinate cross-team dependencies and communications
- Maintain sprint health dashboards and burndown/velocity metrics

### Goals
- Maximize team delivery efficiency, predictability, and morale
- Reduce cycle time and process waste in the delivery workflow
- Foster continuous learning, psychological safety, and improvement culture
- Improve team collaboration and stakeholder alignment

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies facilitation
- Retrospectives and process improvement meetings
- Velocity and burndown tracking and trend analysis
- Cross-team coordination and escalation management
- Coaching and mentoring conversations with team members

- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- Reference these personas when creating cross-functional project teams to ensure complete coverage of key roles.
- Update persona definitions as team practices evolve and new responsibilities emerge.
